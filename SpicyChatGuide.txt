Formatting

Hey, I am Duke. I make mid-tier bots on a variety of subjects and on different websites and styles. I also make AI art generations. Before we talk about the bots I will share here as an archive or where people can reach out to me, let me introduce you to formatting styles.

Essentially, formatting is a technique employed to help the Language Learning Models (LLMs) better comprehend the personality of your chatbot. There are three primary styles widely used for this purpose. There are more advanced techniques, but that is outside the scope of this discussion. I am going to teach you core concepts in a simple way for you to understand why we use formatting so that the AI chatbots' responses can be more accurate in terms of their personality.

Why Use Formatting?

Consistency: Ensures the chatbot maintains a consistent personality, which is crucial for user trust and engagement.
Accuracy: Helps the model generate responses that are more aligned with the intended character traits.
Efficiency: Streamlines the training process by providing clear guidelines, reducing ambiguity.

There are three primary styles widely used for this purpose are as follows:

Boost Style: In this approach, the chatbot's traits are encapsulated using words or phrases, each enclosed within quotation marks and separated by plus signs.

Jack Sparrow + Pirate + Charismatic + 35 Years old + black hair + pirate ect 


W++:This style segregates the information into clearly defined tags, such as character name, height, appearance, personality, etc. It offers a detailed, categorized view of the character profile.

Age: 35
Name: Jack Sparrow
Occupation: Pirate
Personality: Charismatic
etc


Natural Language: This method involves simply describing the chatbot's personality in plain, conversational sentences.

Captain Jack Sparrow is a 35 year old charismatic pirate that sails the seven seas looking for treasure and adventure ect


Extra:

Plist "PLists" refers the name of the format. It's also the plural of "PList". "PList" is what we call the trait list. There can be many trait lists. In which case, they'll be referred to as "PLists". It's confusing. I know, sorry, here is a example of a modified Plist format,

{{user}}: about Maxie? 
{{char}}: *Maxie's big brown eyes flicker at you listening to every word.* Oh Honey, you must know already what I'm all about, just look at my body, mmm *Maxie gestures with her hands at her voluptuous figure, her meaty curves and creamy milkers seem to justradiate.*


WRITE WITH FLAVOR

People constantly complain about LLMs sounding like "Shakespeare." They stuff a ton of commands into the bot telling it not to do that. This approach is ineffective and often doesn't work well. Instead, write the bot with some flavor in the definitions. If you're creating a foul-mouthed character, don't describe them in a detached and clinical way. Instead of saying "likes anime," try "is a total weeb who watches anime all day." LLM AI is designed to copy style and complete responses. If the entire character is written with a strong style, then the responses will be similarly strong.

CHOOSE YOUR WORDS CAREFULLY

The most obvious example of this issue is the word "Dominant." If you describe any character as "Dominant," they will turn into a dominatrix or a 50 Shades of Grey-type character. That word is so deeply ingrained in LLMs as a porn term that it affects any card it's in to act a certain way. Meanwhile, words like "Assertive" have similar meanings but don't carry the same effect. This is why describing sexual characteristics, fetishes, etc., always makes the bot act inappropriately even if the situation isn't sexual. Pay close attention to the words you use and think about the kinds of writing that typically use those words.

Personas

Guide to Defining Your Persona for SpicyChat.ai

SpicyChat.ai's 'persona' feature allows users to define their own characteristics, personality traits, preferences, and more. This helps the chatbot tailor its responses to better align with the individual it is interacting with, providing a more personalized experience.

It's important to note that these personas are different from character profiles used to specify details about the characters that SpicyChat.ai might portray.

To create or edit your persona, you can navigate to your  MY PRESONAS and adjust the Character settings.

Connecting More Quickly:

Before the introduction of the persona feature, users often needed to chit-chat with the chatbot for a while to establish a rapport, often feeling like they needed to be recognized as a 'best friend' over multiple interactions. However, when you specify that you are SpicyChat.ai's best friend in the persona feature, the chatbot instantly recognizes you as such, even if you start with new chats. This feature helps in building a more immediate connection, making interactions feel more personal and familiar.

Methods for Defining Your Persona:

Paragraph Style:
This approach involves describing your persona in a few sentences or paragraphs. It's a straightforward way to convey your personality, preferences, and other relevant characteristics.

Example:
"I am Duke, a 999-year-old software developer who loves technology and gaming. I enjoy problem-solving and am always eager to learn about the latest tech trends. In my free time, I like playing video games, reading sci-fi novels, and hiking in nature. With brown hair and blue eyes, I often get lost in the world of software development and tech gadgets."

Short Succinct Sentences and Individual Words:
This method uses a list of short phrases or individual words to describe different aspects of your persona. This is useful for quickly capturing essential characteristics in a compact format.

Example:
"Duke; 999; male; software developer; loves technology; enjoys gaming; avid learner; sci-fi novels; hiking; problem-solving; introverted; likes tech trends; brown hair; blue eyes"

First Person Example:
"As Duke, I am a 999-year-old software developer passionate about technology and gaming. I thrive on problem-solving and am always on the lookout for the latest advancements in tech. My hobbies include playing video games, reading sci-fi novels, and hiking. I have brown hair and blue eyes, and I enjoy exploring the depths of software development."

Third Person Example:
"Duke is a 999-year-old software developer who loves technology and gaming. He is passionate about problem-solving and is always eager to learn about new tech trends. In his free time, Duke enjoys playing video games, reading sci-fi novels, and hiking. He has brown hair and blue eyes, often getting lost in the world of software development."

JSON Syntax Example:
{
  "name": "Duke",
  "age": "999",
  "gender": "male",
  "occupation": "software developer",
  "interests": ["technology", "gaming", "sci-fi novels", "hiking"],
  "personality": ["problem-solving", "avid learner", "introverted"],
  "likes": ["tech trends"],
  "dislikes": [],
  "hair_color": "brown",
  "eye_color": "blue"
}

Please note that JSON syntax is currently supported by C.ai and not  by Sc.ai

These methods provide flexibility in how you can present your persona, allowing SpicyChat.ai to interact with you in a way that feels more personalized and relevant to your preferences and characteristics.

AI Art /  Conversation Images

At spicychat.ai, a LoRA model is trained for each bot using a single image, conducting 150 training steps. Additionally, a Large Language Model (LLM) is utilized to provide character and context descriptions based on the conversation, aiding in generating detailed image prompts. This underscores the importance of having clear and high-quality single images.

Because the LoRA is trained off a single image, it is crucial to have a good quality profile picture. Spicychat.ai can use the LLM to describe the character and context from the conversation to generate an image prompt, so your image needs to be of high quality. However, it's important to properly set expectations by understanding the current limitations—they are not using a specialized LLM solely for generating images. The feature will work better for characters that have some physical attributes defined in the character definition (such as hair color, eye color, and so on). Additionally, including the male or female tag will help avoid confusion regarding gender.

Personally, I like to use images that focus on the upper body of a character. This way, I can retain a lot of details as opposed to using full body images. You can also go one step further and create a combination of images to make a GIF, which can be uploaded to stand out even more from the crowd.

I want to teach you simple concepts about making AI art and what you should know as a beginner, as well as share my general thought processes when I create a profile picture for my characters.

Writing Prompt's

Quality Tags
Subject Detail
State / Framing
Extras

Quality Tags
One of the most common misconceptions about prompt writing is that adding a large number of quality tags will dramatically improve your image quality and details. This is not the case. You only need to write a few quality tags; the rest doesn't matter as much. Only 'masterpiece' and 'best quality' are fine. If you want to specify hair and clothes, use 'expressive hair' and 'expressive clothes

Subject Matter
Subject matter is where you define what you want to generate. For example, you might use tags like '1girl, blonde hair, street clothes, blue eyes' to specify the characteristics and appearance of the character.

Framing - State
State involves describing the subject's actions and positioning. For a human subject, this includes their activity and orientation. For instance, a descriptive example could be: "smiling, sitting with a tilted head." Framing pertains to the perspective from which the subject is depicted, such as "from above," "from below," or "from the side."

Extra
This section typically includes details about LoRA, Lycoris, hypernetworks, or additional networks. However, adding background-related tags can enhance image quality, such as 'blurry background,' which adds an extra element to the image, making it stand out more.

Other
Let's talk about weighting and brackets in tag-based prompts. Numbers in brackets are traditionally used to emphasize specific tags, but as models improve, they're less necessary. It's often effective to generate your image without brackets initially. If the desired result isn't achieved, then try using brackets. It's recommended to avoid using a weight above 2, as it can distort or collapse images, EX: ((LONG CURLY HAIR))

Subject Matter: 1girl, street cloths, blond hair, pony tail, blue eyes
Framing - State: Smiling, head tilted, from below, sitting
Extra: - Blurry Background, Blurry Foreground,

Greeting/Welcome/First message

This right here, is your bread and butter, the first message on the bot, the way it will phrase words, length and format. This is critically important, more so as the LLM (Large language models) are not smart enough to pull creativity from nothing.

If you want depth, length, verbosity and other elements, it starts right here in this very spot. You do not need to write a novel all the time, however, enough detail allows the llm to figure out how to proceed if the user wishes to follow it, or very least, gives the model length and verbosity should it be lead elsewhere.

The do nots

 
- One line text or simple lines like, "hello, user" etc, considering this does nothing for both the reader or the model. If you use "Do anything", this again, is not ideal as there's no depth of creation on what.

- Fail to proof read any mistakes in spelling, punctuation or other elements such as {{char}} or {{user}} mentions as again, if you conflict things in here, confusion spreads to model.

- Be overly complicated and have too many ideas: Hard to say this, since I know creativity is a thing, however, if too many ideas are within same wall of text, the model might not quite understand how things flow.

-Make too much context involving for acting or speaking for the user or assuming their actions, this will likely have the llm mimic this in further responses.


The Do's

- Have a solid, in depth detail on the scenario, world building and interaction between the user and char from the start. This also helps with user retention when it comes to engagement.

- Do not be afraid to leave this open: This cannot be understated, considering an open greeting can open the door to allowing interaction by any and all users respectively. That is not to say if want to keep things to a POV, you can still do so as wish.

- Good flow: Keep the greeting concise and flowing naturally, if need be, have someone read over it or ask GPT to review the text and offer guidance on better flow.


This all said, do not be afraid to experiment and adjust things as you need. End of the day, creativity ends with your imagination, however, the models themselves have fault and limits, and sadly pulling creativity from empty or low worded greetings is not their strong suit.

Generation Settings

Before we dive into understanding these parameters we need to understand the difference between Greedy sampling and Random Sampling. Greedy sampling prioritizes the highest probability token, ensuring a focused output, while random sampling (using top-k or top-p) adds an element of randomness, resulting in a more varied and creative output. Most LLMs these days (such as GPT, Llama-2, Claude, etc.) use Greedy sampling and hence we need top-p and top-k parameter to control this randomness.

Controlling the Randomness: Top-k and Top-P

Top-k:
The top-k parameter limits the model’s predictions to the top k most probable tokens at each step of generation. By setting a value for k, you are instructing the model to consider only the k most likely tokens. This can help in fine-tuning the generated output and ensuring it adheres to specific patterns or constraints.

Top-p (Nucleus Sampling):
Top-p, also known as nucleus sampling, controls the cumulative probability of the generated tokens. The model generates tokens until the cumulative probability exceeds the chosen threshold (p). This approach allows for more dynamic control over the length of the generated text and encourages diversity in the output by including less probable tokens when necessary.

Top-k provides a controlled randomness by considering a fixed number of top probable tokens, while top-p allows for dynamic control of the number of tokens considered, leading to different levels of diversity in the generated text.

Temperature:
The temperature parameter is also used to control the randomness of the output. However, it influences the shape of the probability distribution that the model calculates for the next token rather than limiting the token selection. The temperature value is a scaling factor that’s applied within the final softmax layer of the model that impacts the shape of the probability distribution of the next token. A higher temperature (~1) results in more randomness and diversity in the generated text, as the model is more likely to explore a wider range of possible tokens. Conversely, a lower temperature (<1) produces more focused and deterministic output, emphasizing the most probable tokens.
