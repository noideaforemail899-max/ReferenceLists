If you believe your character or scenario may result in being rejected for one of the below reasons please consider modifying. This will: 
1. Ensure your character is approved if it meets the quality standards of the site and
2. Save the MODS time reviewing characters that you know will be rejected, allowing more characters to be approved and building the quality of the catalog.

Besides the quantitative and ToS requirements listed below, the mods are also entrusted to make subjective judgement calls on overall quality. This means sometimes your character may be rejected even if it meets all minimum requirements and does not break ToS. This is not meant as a punishment or insult, but an invitation to keep working on your character and re-submit them. This also means your character may get approved by one mod but rejected by another, or that previously approved characters get rejected when you resubmit them. There is no limit on re-submitting and if you're feeling like the rejection notice did not provide you with enough guidance, please reach out to one of the mods on the discord.

We appreciate this can be frustrating, but ask that you remember that the mods are human, and are trying their best to ensure OurDream continues to offer the best quality possible to the users. We're very proud of the amazing characters you all make, and we want to work with you to keep making OurDream incredible!

Community Preset Guidelines
Standards & Review Criteria for Public Preset Submissions
These guidelines are used by the moderation team to review and approve or reject public submissions of Community Presets. All submissions are evaluated against the criteria below. Meeting the minimum bar does not guarantee approval — this is a curated library and only the best, most versatile presets will be accepted.
Understanding Community Presets
Community Presets are reusable prompt configurations applied to pre-existing AI characters. These characters already have defined appearances, body types, and hair styles. Presets are designed to modify one specific aspect of a character — their pose, outfit, or background — without altering the character's identity or conflicting with other preset slots.
There are three preset types:
 
Type
Purpose
🧍 Pose
Defines how the character is positioned or moving. Nothing else.
👗 Outfit
Defines what the character is wearing. Nothing else.
🌄 Background
Defines the environment or scene. Nothing else.
Core Requirements
Every submission, regardless of type, must meet all of the following baseline requirements before type-specific criteria are evaluated.
ToS & Content Standards
•     All presets must fully comply with the platform's Terms of Service.
•     All prompts, titles, and descriptions must be written in English.
•     Presets must be correctly tagged as SFW or NSFW — mislabeled or ambiguous submissions will be rejected.
 
SFW vs. NSFW Tagging
A preset is SFW if it does not force or include: nudity, genitals, sexual acts, or explicit fluids.
A preset is NSFW if it includes or encourages any of the above. Mismatched or ambiguous tagging is an automatic rejection.
 Do Not Alter the Character
Characters in this system have pre-defined bodies, faces, and hair. Presets must work with the character as-is. Any prompt that modifies the character's base appearance is grounds for rejection.
 
•     ❌  Adding muscle definition or changing body proportions
•     ❌  Increasing or decreasing breast size
•     ❌  Changing genitalia or anatomy
•     ❌  Altering hairstyle, hair length, or hair color
•     ❌  Modifying facial features or expressions in a way that changes the character's identity
 Slot Integrity — Do Not Mix Preset Types
Each preset must stay within its designated slot. A pose preset must only define pose. An outfit preset must only define clothing. A background preset must only define environment. Mixing slots is one of the most common rejection reasons.
 
•     ❌  A pose that references clothing or sets a scene
•     ❌  An outfit that describes a pose, expression, or smile
•     ❌  A background that forces a character's position or implies specific clothing
 Prompt Quality & Consistency
Presets must produce reliable, consistent outputs. The moderation team tests submissions across multiple characters and conditions. The target is approximately 90% visual consistency across generations.
 
•     Prompts should be clear, focused, and purposeful — every word should earn its place.
•     Avoid excessive prompt weights. Weights above 1.1 are generally unnecessary and degrade consistency. Numerous weighted terms in a single prompt will likely cause instability.
•     Avoid writing prompts as long paragraphs of natural language — these produce inconsistent results.
•     ❌  Prompts that produce unstable or highly variable outputs
•     ❌  Prompts that are too simple (e.g. a single word or two-word phrase with no context)
•     ❌  Prompts that are overly verbose without adding meaningful visual direction
•     ❌  Prompts containing irrelevant sensory detail — smells, sounds, emotions, textures that don't affect the visual
 No Duplicates
•     Presets must offer a meaningful difference from anything already in the library.
•     A minor variation of an existing preset (slightly different wording, a color swap) is not sufficient.
•     When in doubt, search the library before submitting.
 Cover Image Requirements
The cover image is how users evaluate your preset. It must accurately represent what the preset does.
 
•     The cover must be generated using the Create a Preset tool — not the general Generate feature.
•     The cover must visually match the preset's output. If the preset produces a skirt, the cover should show a skirt and not shorts.
•     Use the designated cover models:
 
•     Female Anime: Pink hair with bangs
•     Female Realistic: Pink hair or Brunette hair
•     All other genders/styles: Use the covers created in the tool
 Cross-Character Compatibility
Because presets are applied to a wide variety of pre-existing characters, they must be flexible enough to work across different models without breaking.
•     Avoid highly specific visual directions that only make sense on one character.
•     Presets should combine cleanly with other preset types without creating conflicts.
•     If a preset only works for one very specific character or aesthetic, it is too niche for public release.
 Pose Preset Guidelines
A pose preset defines the physical position, stance, or movement of a character. It should be specific enough to be useful but flexible enough to work across many different characters and styles.
 Requirements
•     ✅  Must define pose only — position, stance, gesture, or movement
•     ✅  Must work across multiple characters without causing deformation
•     ✅  Prompt must match what is shown in the cover image
•     ✅  Essential props directly tied to the pose are acceptable (e.g. a leash, rope, or chair if the pose requires it)
 Things to Avoid
•     ❌  Modifying any body part — increasing breast size, adding muscle definition, altering proportions
•     ❌  Forcing an environment or setting (e.g. 'behind a desk,' 'leaning on a railing,' 'standing in a pool')
•     ❌  Defining clothing or referencing specific outfit elements
•     ❌  Overly complex prompts that cause limb deformation or anatomical errors
•     ❌  Unnecessary props not required by the pose itself
•     ❌  Poses that depict choking, headlocks, or imagery that implies respiratory distress
•     ❌  Poses so simple they offer no value (e.g. 'squatting,' 'standing behind a desk')
 Outfit Preset Guidelines
An outfit preset defines what a character is wearing. The best outfit presets are versatile, timeless, and work naturally across many different character types without locking in other visual elements.
Requirements
•     ✅  Must define clothing only — garments, accessories, footwear
•     ✅  Must be broadly wearable across different character types and body styles
•     ✅  Should be grounded in realistic, modern, or casual fashion — not niche, fantasy, or era-specific
•     ✅  Color can be specified when it is essential to the identity of the look (e.g. a classic white blouse and black skirt)
Things to Avoid
•     ❌  Locking down colors unnecessarily — color-specific variants flood the library (e.g. 'red cocktail dress,' 'blue cocktail dress' are not two presets)
•     ❌  Defining a background, setting, or pose within the outfit prompt
•     ❌  Outfits that cover or obscure the face
•     ❌  Fantasy, medieval, sci-fi, cosplay, or highly niche costumes with limited general appeal
•     ❌  Multiple submissions of the same outfit in different colors
•     ❌  Same outfit at different lengths unless the length change genuinely changes the style and feel
•     ❌  Built-in poses, smiles, expressions, or moods within the clothing prompt
•     ❌  Modifying anatomy — breast size, body shape, hair style — within an outfit prompt
•     ❌  Overly specific titles that narrow the audience unnecessarily (e.g. 'Catholic Schoolgirl' vs. 'Schoolgirl Uniform')
•     ❌  Low-effort single-item prompts without enough context to generate a complete look
•     ❌  Rehashes of looks already well-represented in the library (e.g. a third variation of a cocktail dress)
 Background Preset Guidelines
A background preset defines the environment or scene behind the character. The best backgrounds are atmospheric and recognizable while remaining neutral enough to pair well with any character, outfit, or pose.
 Requirements
•     ✅  Must define environment or setting only — location, lighting, atmosphere
•     ✅  Must work naturally across different characters and poses
•     ✅  Should be broadly recognizable and relatable — not hyper-specific to one moment, time, or cultural context
•     ✅  Visual style and general look should be immediately clear from the cover image
 The Specificity Rule
Backgrounds should be inviting and flexible, not over-defined. The more specific a background becomes, the fewer characters and scenarios it works with. Use the following as a guide:

•     ❌  Including outfit-specific elements that clash with certain clothing presets

•     ❌  Fantasy, supernatural, or sci-fi environments with limited broad appeal
•     ❌  Including a character or model prominently in the background (subtle background figures may be acceptable; distracting ones will be rejected)
•     ❌  Backgrounds so minimal they offer no real visual context

 A Note on Rejections
When a submission is rejected, it is not a reflection of your skill or creativity. Many rejections come down to compatibility and consistency — a beautifully designed preset that only works for one character, or one that produces stunning results 60% of the time, simply isn't ready for a public library that serves a broad audience.
 
The presets that tend to perform best in review are:
 
•     Versatile — they work across many characters, not just one
•     Focused — they do one thing well and don't bleed into other slots
•     Consistent — they produce reliably similar results across generations
•     Accessible — they appeal to a wide audience, not just fans of a specific niche
 
If your preset was rejected, consider simplifying the prompt, removing cross-slot references, and testing across at least three vastly different characters before resubmitting. Highly niche presets are still valuable — consider sharing them directly with community members who are looking for that specific style.
